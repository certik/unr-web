\section{Introduction}

We did lots of problems with derivatives from the section 3.1, basically the
following rules:
$$(x^n)' = nx^{n-1}$$
$$(cf(x))' = cf'(x)$$
$$(e^x)' = e^x$$
$$(f\pm g)' = f'\pm g'$$

and we also noted all the different ways of denoting a derivative:
$$f'(x)
=f'
={\d\over\d x}f(x)
={\d\over\d x}(f(x))
={\d\over\d x}f
={\d f\over\d x}
$$
